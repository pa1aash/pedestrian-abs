# R3.3 External Comparison Paragraph

Drop-in for Section 4.6:

To contextualise our framework's behaviour against a mature published simulator, we compared C1 and C4 against JuPedSim v1.3.2 at the $w=1.0$\,m bottleneck (50 agents, $n=25$ seeds, tool-default parameters). JuPedSim offers two steering models: a SocialForce (SF) implementation and a CollisionFreeSpeed (CFS) model. JuPedSim SF --- the closest analogue to our C1 --- evacuated only 31.3/50 agents within the 60\,s window (0/25 seeds completed), compared to 50/50 for our C1 (mean 56.5$\pm$5.5\,s). This gap is attributable to JuPedSim's lower default desired speed (0.8 vs.\ 1.34\,m/s) and larger agent radius (0.3 vs.\ 0.25\,m), which reduce throughput at the exit throat. JuPedSim CFS --- a velocity-based model analogous to our ORCA component --- evacuates in 33.6$\pm$1.2\,s, 34\% faster than our C4 (50.8$\pm$4.4\,s, paired Wilcoxon signed-rank $p_{\mathrm{HS}} = 0.000$). The parameter gap produces opposite-direction deviations for the two JuPedSim models: JuPedSim CFS evacuates 34\% faster than our C4 while JuPedSim SF fails to complete evacuation within 60\,s (31.3/50 agents versus our C1's 50/50), illustrating that identical parameter differences can hurt or help evacuation depending on the steering paradigm. The qualitative ordering --- velocity-based models evacuate faster than force-based models at moderate bottleneck widths --- is consistent across both frameworks, supporting the interpretation that this is a property of the steering paradigms rather than an implementation artefact. JuPedSim's C++ core runs approximately 39$\times$ faster than our pure-Python implementation; this well-understood performance gap does not affect the diagnostic contributions of this paper. We note that per-run evacuation times differ by 34\%; we attribute this primarily to the parameter differences noted above (desired speed, agent radius), which do not affect the qualitative ordering of C1 $<$ C4 on deadlock resolution and collision reduction.
